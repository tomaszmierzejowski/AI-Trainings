It assumes the consultants read your code.

They didn't. Not all of it.

The findings are approximately true.

"Approximately" is doing enormous work in that sentence.

Here is a sample of what that found — that traditional assessments had missed:

— 529 API endpoints with no authentication. In one platform. Nobody on the team knew they existed.
— 98 integration connections on a platform documented as having 30.
— Business logic embedded in 17 modules that no single person could enumerate manually.

These are not edge cases. They are typical.

Your platform almost certainly contains findings like this. The question is not whether they are there.
