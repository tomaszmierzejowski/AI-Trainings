In January, I received a forwarded message.

It was from a researcher named Birgit. She had written it the moment she found out her app was working again.

It was Christmas Eve.

Barnas Plattform. A national Norwegian research program studying children's self-esteem across kindergartens. An Android-only app built on Xamarin — a platform that reached end-of-life in 2024. Google Play was deprecating Xamarin support and blocking updates. The January launch was days away. Hundreds of families were about to lose access to something that genuinely mattered.

We had four days.

I won't pretend there wasn't a moment on day two where the build was failing and four days felt like the wrong promise.

We delivered. 92% of the existing code preserved. 1,200+ automated transformations applied. 23 decision points validated manually by a senior architect. Xamarin to .NET MAUI in four days. Google Play — first submission. Zero regressions. 15% performance improvement.

Here is what Birgit wrote:

"It's hard to describe how much relief and joy I felt at that moment. This means that our major research project 'Growing up with good self-esteem in the bag!' can now go as planned."

Not every project is about enterprise architecture and cost savings. Sometimes it is about making sure children and families don't lose access to something that matters.

Cost avoided: €80,000+ emergency rewrite. The platform deadline that would have killed the research program — met.

Four days.

Every number verified. Every finding traced to source code.
