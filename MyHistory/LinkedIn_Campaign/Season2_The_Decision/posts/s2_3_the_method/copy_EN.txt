"Full codebase analysis" sounds like magic until you see the calendar.

Here is what the 3 weeks actually look like, from a real engagement — 20 repos treated as one system:

WEEK 1 — Ingest and map. Every repository loaded. AI-accelerated analysis reads every file and produces the raw map: applications, endpoints, integrations, data flows. In this engagement that meant 187 endpoints mapped across 20 repos. My job this week: configure, sanity-check, catch artifacts early.

WEEK 2 — Verify and dig. This is where the architect earns the fee. Every significant finding gets checked against source, and claims that don't survive verification get killed — in one 97-project analysis the delivered result contained zero erroneous conclusions, because week 2 is where wrong conclusions are supposed to die. The surprises surface now: the integrations nobody documented, the endpoints nobody secured.

WEEK 3 — Reconstruct and deliver. Business rules extracted and confidence-scored, findings written up with source references — 312 evidence-tagged citations in one delivery. The readout isn't a slide show; it's a walkthrough where every claim can be clicked down to the line of code that proves it.

That's it. No magic — a method, a machine that reads everything, and an architect who trusts nothing until verified.

Traditional timeline for the same scope: ~14 weeks. Cost on a comparable engagement: 500K vs 2.3M NOK (discovery and analysis phase only).
