Your CFO doesn't reject modernization budgets. Your CFO rejects unpriced uncertainty.

I've watched heads of engineering lose the same budget conversation for years. The engineer says: "The platform is risky, we need an assessment." The CFO hears: "Give me money to find out if I need more money."

Here is the same request, translated into CFO:

"We are operating an asset nobody has fully inventoried. In one enterprise platform we analyzed, full-coverage analysis found 529 API endpoints with no authentication — under coverage that audits had signed off for years. Each one is a potential regulatory event; GDPR fines alone reach 4% of global revenue.

The inventory costs 500K NOK — a comparable engagement was quoted 2.3M the traditional way (discovery and analysis phase only). Three weeks, fixed scope. It produces an asset register: every endpoint, every integration, every business rule, with evidence. Whatever we decide afterwards — modernize, replace, or do nothing — we decide it on inventory instead of folklore.

The alternative isn't saving 500K. It's continuing to carry an unpriced liability on infrastructure that runs our revenue."

Notice what the translation does: it never asks the CFO to fund a fix. It asks them to price a risk — which is literally their job description.

Heads of engineering, architects: what's the phrase that has actually worked for you in a budget room?
