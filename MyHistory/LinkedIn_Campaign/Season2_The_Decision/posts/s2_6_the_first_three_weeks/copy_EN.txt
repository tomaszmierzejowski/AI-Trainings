Ten posts ago I told you nobody had ever read your code. This post is about what happens if you decide someone finally should.

It's not a pitch — it's a preview of the commitment, in both directions.

What you commit to:
- Read-only repository access before day 1 (the paperwork is the real critical path)
- One senior engineer, ~2 hours a week, to confirm or contest what we flag
- A decision-maker in the room at week 3 — the walkthrough is where decisions happen

What we commit to:
- 100% of files scanned, not sampled
- Every finding cited to file and line — challenge any of them
- Anything we didn't examine, listed explicitly
- Estimates labeled as estimates, in the report and afterwards
- If, after the first conversation, our approach doesn't fit your situation — we say so in writing and you've spent nothing

Three weeks later you own an inventory: every endpoint, every integration, every business rule with a confidence score. What you do with it — modernize, replace, negotiate with a vendor, or nothing — is a decision you make on facts, with us or without us.

That's the whole offer. If your platform has been on your mind through this series, DM me one sentence about it this week. Conversations, not contracts.

And if you're not there yet — the Thursday posts continue. The next series starts with the question I get asked most: "Where would we even begin?"
