Here's a question your last assessment vendor hoped you'd never ask:

"What percentage of the codebase did you actually read?"

I've seen the honest answer for a typical 3-week discovery sprint. It was 12%.

Not because the consultants were lazy. Because reading 100% manually is economically impossible — at consultant reading speed, a 2-million-line platform is years of billable time. So the industry standardized on sampling: read the "important" parts, interview the team about the rest, extrapolate.

The problem isn't the 12%. The problem is what the report did next: presented conclusions about 100% of the platform, with no coverage disclosure anywhere in 160 slides.

Imagine an auditor who checked 12% of transactions and issued an unqualified opinion on the whole ledger. You wouldn't accept it from finance. IT accepts it every year.

The self-test — pull up your last assessment report and look for:

1. A coverage statement (what % of files was scanned, not "reviewed")
2. Source references (findings that cite files and lines, not interviews)
3. A list of what was NOT examined

Zero out of three is the industry standard. That's not an accusation of your vendor. It's the reason we built a different method.
