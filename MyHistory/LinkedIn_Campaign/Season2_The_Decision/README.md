### Why a Season 2, and why this shape

Season 1 (Posts 0–7) built the *realization*: assessments are partial by design, full codebase reading exists, here's the proof. Its job was awareness and credibility.

Season 2 has a different job: **helping Stefan act on the realization.** A VP Engineering who believes us still has to (a) recognize the risk in his own building, (b) qualify vendors, (c) sell the decision internally to a CFO, and (d) know what saying yes actually commits him to. Each Season 2 post removes one of those four blockers. This is buying enablement disguised as content — the highest-converting B2B content class, because the reader can *use* it even if he never buys.

Format shift from Season 1: shorter posts (150–250 words), one idea each, every post ends with a genuine question, every post has a text-bearing graphic (see each post's `graphic_brief.txt`).

### Sequencing rules

- Season 2 starts the Thursday after Post 7 publishes. Never run two conversion posts (S2-6, Post 7) within 4 weeks of each other.
- S2-4 must publish AFTER Post 7 has been live ≥2 weeks — Post 7's DM offer ("I'll send you the 10 questions") needs its exclusivity window before the list goes public. Going public then is deliberate: second exposure for non-DMers, and proof that the DM offer was real.
- PL versions follow the Season 1 pattern: same-week Friday, PL copy in each post folder.

### Voice rules — unchanged from Season 1, plus two new ones

1. Every impressive sentence is followed by the number that makes it real.
2. Write to Stefan at 3am. Short declarative sentences.
3. AI appears once per post, as a tool, never the subject.
4. **NEW:** Every post ends with a question a VP Engineering could answer from memory, in one sentence, without confessing anything sensitive publicly.
5. **NEW:** Never open with context. Open with the wound.
