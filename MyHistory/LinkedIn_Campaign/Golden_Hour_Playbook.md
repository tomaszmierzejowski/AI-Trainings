# Golden Hour Playbook
## The 90-minute protocol for every post

---

### Setup (night before)

- [ ] Post copy open in a tab (from copy_EN.txt)
- [ ] First comment copy open in a second tab (from first_comment_EN.txt)
- [ ] Engagement triggers printed or open (from engagement_triggers.txt)
- [ ] 5 specific colleagues messaged via Teams: post goes live at 08:30, please comment within 60 minutes
- [ ] Calendar blocked: 08:00–10:00 AM — no meetings

---

### The 90-Minute Timeline

**MINUTE 0 — Post goes live**
- Publish between 08:00–08:30 CET
- Screenshot the confirmation for tracking

**MINUTES 1–3 — First comment**
- Open first_comment_EN.txt
- Paste as a comment on your own post immediately
- Do NOT include external links in the post body — links go in the first comment only

**MINUTES 3–8 — Warm DMs**
- Send to 5 high-value connections who haven't seen the post yet
- Use this template: "[Name] — posted something at 08:30 this morning about [one-sentence description relevant to their role]. Thought it would land differently for someone in your position. [link]"
- Prioritize by title: CTO > VP Engineering > Enterprise Architect > CIO > CISO

**MINUTES 8–20 — Colleague coordination**
- Check Teams channel — confirm 3+ colleagues are planning to comment
- If no confirmation: DM them directly with specific angle to take
- Remind them: 30–80 words, genuine perspective, no "great post"

**MINUTES 20–60 — Active engagement**
- Reply to every comment within 15 minutes
- Minimum 20 words per reply
- Use engagement_triggers.txt for substantive comment prompts
- For target-title commenters (CTO, VP Eng, CISO, Enterprise Architect): reply substantively AND send a connection request or DM within the same session

**MINUTES 60–90 — Assessment and rescue**
- Check impression count
- If under the threshold in underperformance_rescue.txt: execute Action 1 immediately
- Identify all target-title engagers — add to outreach queue for same-day DM

---

### Reply Like a Human — rules that override every script below

Learned the hard way (July 2026, Mindset campaign): an Engineering Manager publicly reverse-engineered the reply pattern "validation → metaphor → follow-up question" after two consecutive replies followed it. Readers in IT detect LLM-shaped replies fast, and the reputational cost is real.

1. The scripts below are STARTING ANGLES, not templates. Never paste; retype in your own words.
2. Do NOT end every reply with a question. Vary: sometimes agree and stop, sometimes add one detail, sometimes just thank. A reply chain dies more gracefully than a bot gets exposed.
3. Vary structure between consecutive replies in the same thread — if the last reply opened with validation, open the next one differently.
4. If someone calls out the pattern anyway: hands up, honest self-deprecating reply, tie it back to the campaign thesis (human judgment on top of AI output). It worked once; it only works if it's true.

### Response Scripts by Comment Type

**THE COMPLIMENT ("Great post!" / "Really valuable!")**
Do not respond with "Thank you!" alone. Use:
"Appreciate that — [name the specific element you think landed]. The experience that made that concrete was [anonymized reference to one engagement]. One thing I'd add to what I wrote: [new specific detail not in the post]. What's your context — do you work with legacy platforms directly?"

Goal: Transform passive affirmation into a two-way conversation. The algorithm rewards reply chains more than single replies.

---

**THE SKEPTIC ("These numbers seem too good" / "This sounds like marketing")**
"Fair question — and the right one to ask. The specific fact that changes that conclusion: [cite the source line, report reference, or methodology detail]. I'd rather earn the skepticism than dismiss it. If you want the underlying source data for any number in this post, I can share it directly. What specifically would you want to verify?"

Goal: Never defensive. Treat skepticism as the invitation it is. A public rigorous response increases credibility with every reader who sees it.

---

**THE "WE ALREADY DO THIS" ("Our team also does full codebase analysis")**
"Good to hear — genuinely. What's your coverage methodology? I ask because the gap between 'sampling' and 'full analysis' is exactly where the 529 unauthenticated endpoints were hiding in one of our engagements. If you're doing complete coverage, I'd like to understand the approach — there may be something to learn on both sides."

Goal: Turn competitor positioning into a methodology conversation that showcases specificity. Never dismissive.

---

**THE COMPETITOR MENTION ("[Vendor X] does something similar")**
"I know [them]. Different methodology — their approach covers [X] through [Y] as far as I understand it. The distinction worth drawing is [specific methodological differentiator]. Worth comparing directly if you're evaluating options. DM me if useful."

Goal: Acknowledge without disparaging. Remain the peer in the conversation. Never name competitors negatively in public comments.

---

**SILENCE — Zero engagement at minute 90**

Step 1 (Minute 90): DM 3 specific connections by name — people who would genuinely find this post relevant, chosen by title and known interest:
"[Name] — posted something this morning about [specific element relevant to their role]. Not a pitch — just thought the [529 number / Birgit story / 2-year deployment / specific element] would land differently for someone who deals with [their specific context]. [link]"

Step 2 (Hour 3): Add a comment to the post extending the most provocative element with a technical detail not in the original copy. This reactivates the algorithm without re-posting.

Step 3 (Day 2, if still under 100 impressions): Execute paid boost from underperformance_rescue.txt.

---

### Target-Title Definition

Count these as "target-title engagers" — they trigger the post-golden-hour outreach:
- CTO, CIO, VP Engineering, VP Technology
- Enterprise Architect, Solution Architect, Chief Architect
- Head of Platform, Head of Digital, Transformation Director
- CISO, Head of Compliance, Head of Security
- Engineering Manager at companies with 500+ employees

Do NOT count: colleagues, recruiters, marketing vendors, "great post" replies, students.

### After Golden Hour — Outreach Queue

Within 4 hours of each post:

| Trigger | Action | Timing |
|---------|--------|--------|
| Target-title person likes your post | Send connection request using Connection Request template | Within 4 hours |
| Target-title person comments | Reply substantively → send connection request same day | Reply in golden hour; request same day |
| Target-title person shares your post | Send connection request + thank-you message | Same day |
| Connection accepted | Send value message (not a pitch) | Within 24 hours |
| They reply positively | Send verified results summary (proof PDF) | Same day |
