## PART 1 — HONEST DIAGNOSIS: why Posts 0–4 got little feedback

Low engagement is data. Five posts is enough to read the pattern, and the pattern points at five fixable causes — most of them distribution, not copy:

**1. Audience–network mismatch (the big one).**
The campaign is written for Stefan — a Nordic VP Engineering. But a feed post reaches your existing network first, and only travels beyond it if that network engages. If your connections are mostly Polish colleagues and delivery peers, LinkedIn showed Stefan-content to non-Stefans, got a shrug, and stopped distributing. No hook fixes targeting. Distribution has to be built by hand (see Part 3).

**2. Hooks spent the fold on context.**
LinkedIn cuts posts at ~200 characters on mobile. Post 5's old opener used its entire visible preview on scene-setting ("A government research organization had a mission-critical system their team…"). The v2 rewrites put the wound in the first line, every time.

**3. Posts ended instead of opening.**
Comments are the algorithm's strongest ranking signal, and Posts 0–7 (v1) mostly ended with statements or rhetorical questions no executive would publicly answer ("The question is who gets to read them first"). Every rewritten and new post now ends with a question that can be answered in one honest sentence without confessing anything sensitive.

**4. The posts looked like marketing to the exact person they target.**
Stefan "skips anything that sounds like marketing" — his persona file says so. Five hashtags including #SopraSteria, a repeated tagline, zero vulnerability. v2 cuts to 3 hashtags, keeps the tagline once as a series signature, and adds confession-pattern hooks ("For 14 years, I read maybe 20%…") that cost the author something. Authority without vulnerability reads as advertising.

**5. One-way cadence, no warm-up executed.**
The Publishing_Calendar's warm-up DMs and colleague briefings are the actual distribution engine for a small network — if they weren't executed (or were executed once), posts launched cold. In v2 the warm-up is non-optional: a post without its warm-up moves to the next slot.

What was NOT the problem: the strategy, the story arc, the evidence discipline, or the volume of material. Don't throw those away. The campaign has the rare thing — verifiable proof. It needs a delivery system.

| Day | Time (CET) | Track | Language |

| **Wednesday** | 08:30 | AI MINDSET — PL version of Tuesday's post | PL |

| **Friday** (optional) | 08:30 | AI MODERNIZATION — PL version | PL |

**Why this split works strategically:**
- Two audiences, one persona. Tuesday sells behavior change to leaders and professionals (training buyers — largely Polish, hence PL the next day is near-mandatory). Thursday sells codebase truth to enterprise engineering leaders (largely international — EN primary, PL optional by capacity).
- The tracks reinforce, not repeat: Tuesday teaches the mindset; Thursday shows the mindset at enterprise scale. Cross-references are planted (M3's first comment points to Thursdays; S2-1 and M7 mirror each other's "one person who knows" risk). One worldview, two altitudes — that's a personal brand, and later, a TikTok channel.
- Friday PL Modernization is explicitly OPTIONAL. Three quality golden hours a week beats four rushed ones. Drop the Friday slot first whenever capacity is tight.

**Sustainability rule:** each published post costs ~2 hours all-in (final read, publish, first comment, 90-minute golden hour, day-after DMs). Budget ~6 h/week. If a week can't afford a post, skip the slot — a gap hurts less than an unattended post.

### Standing weekly mechanics (non-negotiable — this is the engagement fix)

1. **Warm-up (day before, 10 min):** 5–8 DMs to people for whom tomorrow's post is personally relevant. Script: "[Name] — tomorrow 08:30 I'm posting about [specific pain]. Thought of you because [specific reason]. Would genuinely value your take in the comments." Personal relevance, never "please support."
2. **Runway (15 min before posting):** comment substantively on 5 posts by people in the target audience. LinkedIn boosts posts from accounts that were just active in others' threads.
3. **First comment:** paste within 60 seconds of publishing (it's pre-written in every post folder).
4. **Golden hour (90 min):** reply to every comment within minutes, and END EVERY REPLY WITH A QUESTION to the commenter. A 2-comment thread counts double; a 10-comment thread changes the post's reach class. Full protocol: `Golden_Hour_Playbook.md`.
5. **Day after (15 min):** DM engagers matching the target persona (templates: `DM_Templates.md`). This is where posts become pipeline — reach is vanity, DMs are revenue.
6. **Colleague amplification:** 3–5 colleagues briefed to comment (not just react) within the first hour. Comments from outside your first-degree network are worth the most — prioritize briefing colleagues in other countries' Sopra entities.
7. **Personal profile always.** Company pages get throttled; people follow people. Ask the Sopra Steria Poland page to reshare ~2 hours after publish, never simultaneously.

### A/B and rescue discipline

- Every post folder has `hook_variants.txt` with a recommendation and the reasoning. If a post is under ~50% of your rolling median impressions at hour 8: apply its `underperformance_rescue.txt` (Season 1) or add a self-reply comment with the strongest unused hook variant + one new fact (Season 2/Mindset).
- Change ONE variable per retry. Log every post in the tracker below — after 6 weeks the data will say which hook patterns your audience buys. Follow the data over this document.

## PART 4 — TIKTOK SEED PLAN (build now, launch later)

Every post in both campaigns was written video-first: each `hook_variants.txt` flags its best spoken hook, and each graphic doubles as a 9:16 cover frame. When the TikTok channel launches, the scripts already exist:

- **Format:** 30–60s talking head, hook in the first 2 seconds (use the flagged variant), one idea, one on-screen number, end question. No intro, no logo bumper.
- **Batch protocol:** record 4–6 in one session, same shirt = same series identity. Publish 2–3/week; repost the best to LinkedIn as native video (video is currently LinkedIn's most-favored format and none of the 17 posts uses it yet — the TikTok cuts become LinkedIn's video layer for free).
- **Priority order for first recordings** (chosen for spoken-hook strength): M4 ("I hide three lies in reports"), M8-C ("My success metric: you never book me again"), S2-2-B ("An auditor who checked 12%…"), Post 5-B ("Two years blocked. One month to fix."), M2 (the staircase, pointing at levels), M7-B (the laptop bag).
- **Language:** TikTok channel should be PL-first (the training market), with EN subtitles burned in — this doubles as the LinkedIn video asset.
- Keep every recording's raw file; the same material later becomes YouTube Shorts and the training program's marketing reel.
