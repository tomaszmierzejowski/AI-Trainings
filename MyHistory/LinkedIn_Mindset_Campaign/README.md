# LinkedIn Campaign — THE ORDER
## AI Mindset | Tuesdays | Tomasz Mierzejowski / Sopra Steria

---

### The Big Idea

Everyone is getting AI tool training. Almost nobody is changing behavior. The difference between people AI helps and people AI doesn't isn't the tools — **it's the order in which they work.** This campaign sells that single idea eight ways, and behind it, the AI-Mindset training program.

### The Enemy

The Tool Tour — the industry's default AI training: two hours of features, a list of prompts, zero behavior change by Monday morning. (Mirror of the Modernization campaign's enemy, the Discovery Slide Deck. Both enemies are *performances of competence that change nothing*.)

### Target Reader

Two, deliberately:

**Renata, 41** — Managing Director of a 50–200 person non-IT company (agency, services, events, logistics). Bought ChatGPT licenses eight months ago. Usage peaked in week two. She suspects her people are embarrassed to admit they don't know what to do with it. She would never book "AI training" again — but she would book behavior change.

**Paweł, 35** — team lead / senior professional. Uses AI daily but as a polisher, not a first step. Doesn't know he's Level 2. Will self-diagnose the moment he sees the Spectrum. He's the reshare engine that reaches Renata.

### Relationship to the Thursday campaign

Thursday (Modernization) proves what an AI-augmented expert does at enterprise scale. Tuesday (Mindset) teaches the behavior that makes it possible. They cross-reference but never depend on each other. One person, one worldview, two altitudes — this is deliberate personal-brand architecture, and it is also the TikTok content engine later.

### Voice Rules

1. Every claim carries its receipt — a study citation or "from a session I ran." The evidence-badge discipline (VERIFIED / STUDY / OPINION) is itself part of the brand: if a number has no badge, challenge me.
2. Write to Renata on a Tuesday morning — no AI jargon, no model names in the copy body (tools may appear in comments).
3. Stories start with people, never with technology. The Domek story starts with the kids, not the prompt.
4. Every post ends with a question answerable in one honest sentence.
5. Self-deprecating beats self-congratulating. The trainer who shows his own AI corrections outranks the guru who doesn't.

### Numbers cleared for public use (with badges)

- STUDY: Peng et al. 2023 (GitHub/MIT RCT) — task completed 55.8% faster with AI assistance
- STUDY: Cui et al. (Management Science, 4,867 developers) — 26% more completed tasks; adoption behavior, not seniority, predicted the benefit
- STUDY: McKinsey 2023 — gains materialize only with training and practice; minimal gain on highly complex tasks
- STUDY: Lally et al. 2010 — new habits take ~66 days on average, not 21
- STUDY: Gollwitzer & Sheeran 2006 — implementation intentions ("when X, I will Y"), effect size d=0.65
- FRAMEWORK: Fogg Behavior Model (B=MAP); habit loop; status-quo bias (Kahneman & Tversky)
