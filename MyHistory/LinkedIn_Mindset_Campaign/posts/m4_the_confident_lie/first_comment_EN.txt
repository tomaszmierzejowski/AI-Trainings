The pattern behind error #3, for anyone who wants the mechanism:

An inverted conclusion works because it recruits three allies — fluent prose (signals competence), a true statistic (signals rigor), and our own hope (nobody WANTS the numbers to be bad). Psychologists call the umbrella effect automation bias: we under-scrutinize output that arrives looking authoritative.

The defense is structural, not motivational. You can't tell people "be more careful" — carefulness doesn't survive a busy Tuesday. You give them an order of checking that catches the worst first. That 60-second sequence is next week's post.
