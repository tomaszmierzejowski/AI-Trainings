To be clear about the hero themselves: they're usually the best person in the building, and none of this is their fault. Punishing or sidelining them is the dumbest possible response.

The move is to change their job description from doing to teaching: their new deliverable is "who did this without me this month." Same status, same visibility — pointed at multiplication instead of accumulation. Most heroes are relieved; being the only one was never comfortable.

And a measurement note for leaders: if you track anything, track behavior, not sentiment. First-step rate (what share of tasks start with AI), verification rate (what share of AI output gets checked before it ships). If people game the first-step rate by... using AI first on real tasks — congratulations, that's adoption.
