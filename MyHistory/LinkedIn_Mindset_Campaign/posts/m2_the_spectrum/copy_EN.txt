Those four answers are the four levels of working with AI. Each has a name and its own economics:

LEVEL 1 — Curiosity. "I tried ChatGPT once." Maybe twice. It was interesting. Nothing changed.

LEVEL 2 — Polish. AI gets finished work to improve, whenever I remember it exists. A smarter search box, part-time proofreader.

LEVEL 3 — Draft first. Every report, analysis and plan starts with an AI draft and ends with my judgment. AI is the first step. Not the only step. The first.

LEVEL 4 — Design. I'm not doing the task anymore. I design a process that AI runs end-to-end, and I verify the results.

Two patterns show up every time I discuss this:

1. Almost everyone places themselves one level higher than their actual behavior last Monday.

2. The jump that actually changes the economics of your work is 2 → 3. It needs no new tools, no budget, no permission. It needs one changed reflex.

Level 4 isn't the goal for most of us. Level 3 is. Habit research says a new reflex becomes automatic after ~66 days on average (Lally et al., 2010) — but the difference in pace shows up in week one.
