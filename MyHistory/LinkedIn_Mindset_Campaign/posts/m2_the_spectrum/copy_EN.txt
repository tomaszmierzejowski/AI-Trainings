Those four answers are the four levels of working with AI, and each has its own economics.

LEVEL 1 — Curiosity. "I tried ChatGPT once." Maybe twice. It was interesting; nothing changed.

LEVEL 2 — Polish. AI gets finished work to improve, whenever I remember it exists. A smarter search box, part-time proofreader.

LEVEL 3 — Draft first. Every report, analysis and plan starts with an AI draft and ends with my judgment. AI is the first step — not the only one.

LEVEL 4 — Design. I'm not doing the task anymore. I design a process that AI runs end-to-end, and I verify the results.

Two patterns show up every time I discuss this. First: almost everyone places themselves one level higher than their actual behavior last Monday. Second: the jump that really changes the economics of your work is 2 → 3 — and it needs no new tools, no budget, no permission. One changed reflex.

Level 4 isn't the goal for most of us; Level 3 is. Habit research says a new reflex becomes automatic after about 66 days on average (Lally et al., 2010), but the difference in pace shows up in week one.

If you write your level in the comments, I'll suggest the fastest first move up from there.
