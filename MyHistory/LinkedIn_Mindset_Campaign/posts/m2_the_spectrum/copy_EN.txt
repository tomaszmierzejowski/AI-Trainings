There are four levels of working with AI. Most professionals are on Level 2 and believe they're on Level 3.

The spectrum I use in every training session:

LEVEL 1 — AI-Curious.
"I tried ChatGPT once." Maybe twice. It was interesting. Nothing changed.

LEVEL 2 — AI-Assisted.
"I use AI when I remember to." It polishes finished work. It answers occasional questions. It's a smarter search box.

LEVEL 3 — AI-Augmented.
"AI is my first step to everything." Every draft, every analysis, every plan starts there — and every output passes through your judgment before it counts. Not the only step. The first.

LEVEL 4 — AI-Native.
"I design work that AI does end-to-end. I verify the results." You're not doing the task anymore; you're architecting how it gets done.

Two things I see in every single group:

1. People place themselves one level higher than their actual Monday behavior.
2. The jump that changes careers is 2 → 3. It requires no new tools, no budget, no permission. Only a changed default.

Level 4 isn't the goal for most people. Level 3 is. It's within a month's reach of anyone reading this — the habit research says ~66 days to automatic (Lally et al., 2010), but the economics show up in week one.

Diagnose yourself by behavior, not aspiration:

Where did your last important piece of work START?
