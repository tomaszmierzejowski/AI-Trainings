The objection I hear immediately: "But AI's first draft is mediocre."

Correct. So is a new colleague's first draft — and you don't fire them on day one. You give feedback, and by round three the draft carries your standards.

Nobody's identity is threatened by delegating a first draft to a junior. That's the whole reframe: it was never about the machine's quality. It was about who goes first.
