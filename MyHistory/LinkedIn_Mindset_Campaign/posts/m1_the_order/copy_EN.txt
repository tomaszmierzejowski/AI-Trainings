Two people. Same job. Same AI tools. One gets 10x more out of them.

I've watched this for two years in training rooms, and the difference is never what people expect.

It's not prompting skill. Not technical background. Not age.

It's the order.

Person A finishes the draft, then asks AI to polish it. The report, the offer, the plan — built the hard way first, AI invited in at the end, as decoration.

Person B asks AI for the draft first — then applies their judgment, expertise, and taste to it.

Same tools. Opposite sequence. Radically different economics: Person A saves minutes. Person B saves the part of the work that was never the valuable part anyway — the blank page.

Here's the phrase I teach: AI is your first step to everything. Not the only step. The first.

The research backs the pattern. A field study across 4,867 developers found ~26% more completed tasks with AI — but the gains varied enormously, and what predicted them wasn't seniority. It was adoption behavior (Cui et al., Management Science).

The tools will change again next quarter. The order is yours to keep.

So, honestly: which person are you — draft-first, or polish-last?
