Last week I showed you the lie that survives every audit. Here's the 60 seconds that catches it.

The order matters more than the effort. When people "carefully review" AI output, they read it like an essay — top to bottom, nodding along with the prose. That's exactly how the confident lie gets signed.

Read it like an auditor instead. Sixty seconds, fixed sequence:

SECONDS 0–20 — Names, numbers, dates.
Highest fabrication density in any AI output. Check them against the source, not against your memory. A wrong number is the easiest catch and the fastest credibility test of the whole document.

SECONDS 20–40 — Sources.
Every citation: does it exist? One invented benchmark poisons every real one around it.

SECONDS 40–60 — Conclusion vs. data.
Cover the conclusion paragraph. Look only at the underlying numbers. Now ask: what conclusion would I draw? Uncover. If yours and the document's disagree — you've just caught the error that survives every training room I run.

Then one rule on top: no citation, no claim. Truth lives in your systems of record; AI is the understanding layer on top of them. Anything that can't show its receipt doesn't get forwarded.

Sixty seconds. Not paranoia — procedure.

And here's the reframe I insist on: verification isn't distrust of AI. It's the thing that makes your signature mean something. When you sign, YOU are the claim.
