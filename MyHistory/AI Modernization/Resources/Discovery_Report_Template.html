About Confidence Scores

Every section of this report carries a confidence score computed with the Evidence Coverage Index (ECI): 50% evidence breadth (share of findings backed by Verified or Partial evidence), 35% critical verification (share of Critical and High findings fully verified in code), and 15% analysis coverage (share of relevant files actually analyzed, taken from the engagement's coverage ledger). Scores are rounded to the nearest 10 points (±10-point precision) and mapped to tiers: Very High 90-100 · High 70-89 · Medium 50-69 · Low 30-49 · Very Low 0-29. Sections with few findings show the tier label only.

Individual findings carry an evidence status instead of a number: Verified (confirmed in code with file:line citation), Partial, Assumed, Inferred, or Cannot Determine. Findings tagged MUST VERIFY or SHOULD VERIFY require human confirmation before being relied upon. Section scores indicate where further discovery should focus; per-finding evidence status indicates how much an individual finding can be trusted.
