# Module 1 — The Psychology of the AI Mindset (Slide Outline)

**Format:** 90-minute lecture + discussion · **18 slides** · Audience: mixed (leaders + practitioners)
**Design intent:** story-driven, one idea per slide, large type, minimal bullets. Evidence appears on the same slide as the claim it supports.

---

**Slide 1 — Title**
"AI-Mindset: Changing Behaviors to Thrive in an AI World"
Subtitle: *This is not a tool training.*
Visual: single fishing rod icon (recurring program motif).

**Slide 2 — The wrong question**
Left: "Which AI tool should we buy?" (crossed out)
Right: "What will our people do differently on Monday morning?"
One line: Tools change every quarter. Behavior compounds.

**Slide 3 — Ground rules: evidence over opinion**
Three-badge legend used throughout the program: **VERIFIED** (delivered project, traceable), **STUDY** (cited external research), **FRAMEWORK/OPINION** (labeled as such).
One line: "If a number on these slides has no badge, challenge me."

**Slide 4 — The Domek story**
Photo-style visual: a wooden playhouse sketch.
"My kids wanted a little house. One AI session: architectural drawings, structural calculations, a ready shopping cart at the building store. One hour. No architect."
No metrics on this slide — it's the hook.

**Slide 5 — Same pattern, professional scale** *(VERIFIED)*
Project Terrain: generative AI in physical playground design.
~200 design variants · 8 iteration rounds · 3–4× cost reduction vs. traditional approach.
One line: "Same behavior as the Domek. Different stakes."

**Slide 6 — Same pattern, highest stakes** *(VERIFIED)*
The 4-day rescue: a Norwegian children's-research non-profit, app dead on an EOL framework, national research program at risk.
Xamarin → .NET MAUI in **4 days** (typical: 4–8 weeks) · 92% code preserved · zero regressions · 100% first-submission store approval.
Pull-quote (project lead, Christmas Eve): *"It's hard to describe how much relief and joy I felt at that moment."*

**Slide 7 — The AI-Augmented Spectrum**
Four ascending levels (staircase visual):
Level 1 **AI-Curious** → Level 2 **AI-Assisted** → Level 3 **AI-Augmented** → Level 4 **AI-Native**.
One line: "The target for most people in this room is Level 3."

**Slide 8 — Where are you? (self-diagnosis)**
One behavioral tell per level:
L1: "I tried ChatGPT once." · L2: "I use AI when I remember to." · L3: "AI is my first step to everything. Not the only step. The first." · L4: "I design work AI does end-to-end; I verify."
Interactive: hands up / dot voting.

**Slide 9 — The difference isn't in the tools. It's in the order.**
Full-slide statement. Speaker builds this: same tools, same access — different *sequence of first moves* separates Level 2 from Level 3.

**Slide 10 — Why adoption actually stalls** *(FRAMEWORK)*
The friction map — four named frictions:
1. **Fear of looking incompetent** ("juniors use crutches")
2. **Identity threat** ("my craft is being automated")
3. **Trust miscalibration** (burned once → never again, or blind trust)
4. **Habit inertia** (the old way is zero-effort)
One line: none of these are solved by a feature tour.

**Slide 11 — Your brain is working as designed** *(FRAMEWORK — Kahneman/Tversky)*
Status-quo bias + loss aversion: a proven old workflow feels "safe" even when it's objectively slower; a new workflow's cost is felt immediately, its payoff later.
Label clearly: behavioral-science framework, applied to AI adoption by reasoned extrapolation.

**Slide 12 — What the data actually says** *(STUDY)*
Field experiments across ~4,800+ developers (incl. Microsoft, 2024): ~26% more completed tasks with AI assistance — but with wide variance, and the largest gains among those who *changed how they work*, not the most senior.
One line: "The tool was constant. The behavior was the variable."

**Slide 13 — Why tool training fails**
"Give a fish" model: trainer demos features → participants nod → Monday, old habits win.
Evidence framing: McKinsey (2023) — productivity gains materialize only with training *and practice*; minimal gains on complex tasks without changed working patterns. *(STUDY)*

**Slide 14 — Daję wędkę: I give you a fishing rod**
The program's contract with the audience:
"I will not leave you with my phone number as your AI strategy. You leave with habits, not dependencies. I'll build a fish when I must — to show it's possible — but you leave with the rod."
Visual: rod motif from slide 1, now handed over.

**Slide 15 — Why you can trust the messenger** *(VERIFIED)*
"Nie uczę teorii — I don't teach theory."
14 years of engineering. Project Meridian-Core: 5+ years of continuous modernization of a global mission-critical platform, zero operational interruptions.
One line: "This advice is written by someone who carries production risk."

**Slide 16 — The reframe**
Full-slide statement: **"AI doesn't replace knowledge. It removes excuses for not having it."**
Speaker connects: the Domek needed no architect *because the knowledge became accessible* — the judgment to verify it stayed human.

**Slide 17 — Your Level-3 commitment (exercise)**
Each participant writes one recurring task where AI becomes their *first step* starting tomorrow. Pairs share. Keep the card — it returns in Module 5.

**Slide 18 — Recap + bridge**
Three takeaways: (1) behavior, not tools; (2) name your friction — it's normal and it's addressable; (3) first step, not only step.
Bridge: "Module 2: we make the first step concrete with four tools — Claude, Cursor, NotebookLM, Gemini."
