# Pre-Training Survey (GENERIC — OPTIONAL)

**Status (2026-07-03):** survey postponed for the first customer by author decision — the program is generic with audience tracks (CORE / NON-IT / IT) selectable in the decks. Use this survey only when a client wants a tailored delivery; it is not a delivery prerequisite.

**Purpose (internal):** every answer here upgrades an [INFERRED] item in Module 5 (and the trainer guides) to [VERIFIED — client's own words]. Quote answers verbatim in the customer application block. This survey also drives lab logistics for Module 2 (tool access) and audience split for Module 4.
**Format:** send as a form or short structured interview (20 min). Interview beats form when you can get it — follow-ups are where the real pain points surface.

---

## A. Who is in the room
1. Roles attending (count per role): technical leads / architects / BAs & consultants / sales engineers / delivery managers / executives.
2. Rough split: how many attendees write code weekly? How many never do?
3. Language preference for materials: EN / PL / mixed. *(Program is EN-first; PL variants planned.)*

## B. Your delivery reality (validates Module 5's assumptions — currently INFERRED)
4. In your own words: what are the three biggest sources of friction in building and changing client workflows today?
   *(Our current hypothesis — requirements churn, decision bottlenecks in routing/prioritization, manual intake effort. Confirm, correct, or replace.)*
5. Where do deliveries lose the most calendar time — intake, configuration, integration, testing, client review, approvals, elsewhere?
6. What has your team already tried with AI? What worked, what quietly died, and why (your honest read)?
7. Is there a workflow you'd nominate as the pilot for a "first-step habit" — recurring, painful, owned by people in the room?

## C. Desired outcomes
8. If this training works, what is visibly different for your team 60 days later? (Concrete: an artifact, a metric, a behavior.)
9. What would make it a failure in your eyes — even if the feedback forms are positive?
10. Preferred tangible outputs: ☐ case prototype ☐ prompt/spec library seeded ☐ demo asset for prospects ☐ internal accelerator ☐ other: ____

## D. Tools & governance (drives Module 2 lab logistics — answer blocks delivery if late)
11. Which of these are approved and accessible on attendee machines today: Claude / Cursor / NotebookLM / Gemini? Which are blocked, and is an exception process available for training?
12. What data may attendees use in AI tools during labs? (We default to fictional datasets; if you want labs on your real materials, what's the approval path?)
13. Who owns AI governance on your side (name/role) — may we align with them before delivery?

## E. The people layer (drives Modules 1 & 5)
14. On the four adoption frictions — fear of looking incompetent, identity threat ("my craft is being automated"), trust after being burned, plain habit inertia — which do you observe most on your team? Any examples you can share?
15. Will a senior leader attend Module 5 and commit to the weekly "delegated + corrected" post? *(We reschedule Module 5 rather than run it without leadership — it doesn't work.)*
16. Is there a natural champion candidate — a volunteer enthusiast colleagues already ask for help?

## F. Logistics
17. Preferred format: 3 consecutive days / weekly half-days / other. On-site (client's location), remote, or hybrid?
18. Group size (target: 8–16 for labs).
19. Anything about your client commitments or busy periods we should schedule around?
