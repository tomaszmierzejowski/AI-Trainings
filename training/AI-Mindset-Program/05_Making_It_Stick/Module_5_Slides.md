# Module 5 — Making It Stick: The 30-Day Behavior Plan (Slide Outline)

**Format:** half-day workshop · **12 slides** · Audience: everyone; leaders REQUIRED (slides 6–7 are addressed to them)
**Design intent:** the fewest slides, the most writing-by-participants. This module produces artifacts: personal 30-day plans, team rituals, and a measurement sheet.

---

**Slide 1 — Title**
"Making It Stick: The 30-Day Behavior Plan"
Subtitle: *Training fades. Design doesn't.*

**Slide 2 — The honest problem**
Most training is enthusiasm that decays on contact with the calendar. Knowledge isn't the gap — you now know more than most of your industry. The gap is that the old habits are still cheaper *this week*.
Callback: the four frictions from Module 1 are still in the room.

**Slide 3 — Design beats willpower** *(FRAMEWORK — Fogg Behavior Model)*
B = MAP: behavior happens when Motivation, Ability, and a Prompt coincide. Motivation is unreliable — so we engineer Ability (make it tiny) and Prompts (attach to existing triggers).
One line: "We don't plan to try harder. We redesign the default."

**Slide 4 — The habit ladder (individual)**
Week 1: ONE first-step habit (from your Module 1 card + Module 2 contract) — tiny, attached to an existing trigger. Week 2: add the verification spot-check (Module 3). Week 3: first written spec for a real task (Module 4). Week 4: review the trail — what did AI touch, what did you sign?
One line: one habit per week; a stack, not a leap.

**Slide 5 — Team rituals (make it social)**
Three rituals, adopt at least one today: **AI moment in the daily** (60 seconds: one thing someone delegated yesterday); **shared prompt/spec library** (living, in the repo, not a wiki graveyard); **monthly show-and-tell** (one real workflow, warts included — failures teach adoption faster than wins).

**Slide 6 — Leaders go first** *(addressed to leadership)*
Adoption follows visible seniority behavior, not policy. If leaders delegate to AI openly — and *verify openly* — the fear-of-looking-incompetent friction dies org-wide.
One line: "Your team doesn't adopt what you mandate. They adopt what they see you do."

**Slide 7 — Champions, not police**
A champions network: one volunteer per team, time-budgeted (not "on top of everything"), tasked with unblocking colleagues and harvesting wins for the show-and-tell — never with enforcement.
FRAMEWORK (Rogers, diffusion of innovations): adoption spreads through trusted peers, not through memos.

**Slide 8 — Measure behavior, not sentiment**
Drop "do you feel more productive?" surveys. Track leading indicators: **first-step rate** (share of eligible tasks where AI was step one), **verification rate** (share of AI output that got the 60-second check), **spec reuse** (rules files and specs in the repo, used more than once), **time-to-artifact** on one benchmark task, measured monthly.
One line: "What gets measured gets repeated — so measure the behavior, not the mood."

**Slide 9 — Case application: PlatformCo** *(customer-specific block — replace per client)*
Applying the ladder to PlatformCo's Kraków delivery context (low-code platform delivery: intake, workflow configuration, client demos, presales).
**[INFERRED — validate with client]:** pain points assumed from proposal stage: requirements churn in workflow builds; decision bottlenecks in routing/prioritization; manual intake effort. First-step habits proposed against each.
Footer, visible: *These assumptions come from our pre-engagement analysis, not from PlatformCo's own words — the pre-training survey validates or corrects them.*

**Slide 10 — Anti-patterns (what kills adoption)**
The mandate ("everyone will use AI by Q3") → compliance theater. Tool sprawl (new tool every month) → zero habits. The hero (one enthusiast does everyone's AI work) → single point of failure, no rod handed over. Silent measurement (tracking without telling) → trust collapse.

**Slide 11 — Write your 30-day contract (working block)**
Participants complete the one-page plan: my Level-3 task (Module 1 card) · my tool+trigger (Module 2 contract) · my verification rule (Module 3 matrix quadrant) · my spec candidate (Module 4) · my accountability partner · the date of the day-30 review.
Leaders complete the team sheet: ritual chosen, champion named, three metrics baselined.

**Slide 12 — Close: the rod is yours**
Program recap in one breath: behavior over tools (M1) → first-step habits (M2) → calibrated trust (M3) → specs and orchestration (M4) → designed persistence (M5).
Final statement: "Daję wędkę. I gave you the rod. The 30-day review is yours to run — and if you run it well, you won't need me in the room. That was the point."
Contact + day-30 review template handout.
