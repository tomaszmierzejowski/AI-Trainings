## The ten numbers to know cold

1. **~66 days** — median time to habit automaticity; range 18–254 (Lally et al., 2010). Missing a single day did not derail formation — the basis of the program's "resume, don't restart" rule.
2. **d = 0.65** — mean effect of implementation intentions ("when X, I will Y") across 94 studies (Gollwitzer & Sheeran, 2006). The habit contract's tool+trigger design is this, operationalized.
3. **+26%** — completed tasks in three-firm field experiments with AI coding assistants, ~4,800 developers (Cui et al., 2024); wide variance, biggest gains among less-tenured.
4. **55.8%** — faster completion of a scoped coding task in the Copilot RCT (Peng et al., 2023).
5. **+14% / +34%** — call-center productivity overall / for novices, ~5,000 agents (Brynjolfsson, Li & Raymond, 2023). The strongest "AI compresses skill gaps" evidence.
6. **+12.2% tasks, +25.1% speed, +40% quality — but −19 percentage points outside the frontier** — BCG consultants with GPT-4 (Dell'Acqua et al., 2023, "the jagged frontier"). The single most useful study in the program.
7. **−40% time, +18% quality** — professional writing tasks with ChatGPT (Noy & Zhang, Science, 2023); the gap between weaker and stronger writers narrowed.
8. **−19% (felt +20%)** — experienced open-source developers were slower with AI on their own mature repos while believing they were faster (METR, 2025). The calibration cautionary tale — and proof that time-to-artifact must be measured, not felt.
9. **~31%** — software projects fully successful (Standish CHAOS, 2020). Never "73% fail" — that number exists in no Standish report.
10. **2.5 / 13.5 / 34 / 34 / 16** — Rogers' adopter categories (innovators, early adopters, early majority, late majority, laggards), the % split of the diffusion curve.

## The honesty hierarchy (the program's spine, applied to psychology)

When you present psychology, hold the same badge discipline as everywhere else. From strongest to weakest evidence: **Big Five** (decades of validation, predictive of behavior) → **domain-specific validated instruments** (Technology Readiness Index; Oreg's Resistance to Change scale) → **validated effects** (implementation intentions, algorithm aversion, psychological safety) → **practitioner frameworks** (DISC, PRISM, Kotter, ADKAR, Fogg's B=MAP — useful lenses, weak or absent psychometrics; always labeled MODEL, never sold as science) → **popularizations** (Duhigg's habit loop, Atomic Habits — fine as pedagogy, cite the underlying research when challenged).

Saying "DISC is a communication lens with weak psychometrics — the validated backbone is the Big Five" does not weaken you in front of an audience. It is the single strongest credibility move available, because in every corporate audience there is one person who knows, and that person becomes your ally instead of your ambush.

## Audiobook production note

These files are written to survive text-to-speech: no tables carry unique content that the surrounding prose doesn't restate, abbreviations are expanded on first use per file, and each section opens with a topic sentence. Suggested listening order for first mastery: C1, C3, C4, C2, C5. Before quoting any specific number in print or on camera, re-verify against the original paper — study numbers in secondary sources drift, and this program's brand is that its numbers survive checking.
