The design stance  FRAMEWORK — Fogg: B = MAP

We don’t plan to try harder.
We redesign the default.

  • Behavior happens when Motivation, Ability and a Prompt coincide.
  • Motivation is unreliable. So we engineer the other two:
  • Ability — make the habit tiny. Prompt — attach it to an existing trigger.

Team — make it social

Three rituals. Adopt at least one today.

AI moment in the daily

60 seconds: one thing someone delegated yesterday.

Shared prompt & spec library

Living, where you work — not a wiki graveyard.

Monthly show-and-tell

One real workflow, warts included. Failures teach adoption faster than wins.

Chosen in the room, not suggested. An unchosen ritual is a suggestion.

To the most senior person in the room

Leaders go first

  • Your team doesn’t adopt what you mandate. They adopt what they see you do.
  • The ask: once a week, in a channel everyone reads, post what you delegated to AI — and what you had to correct.
  • The correction is the active ingredient: visible senior fallibility kills the fear-of-looking-incompetent friction org-wide.

The network  FRAMEWORK — Rogers, diffusion of innovations

Champions, not police

  • One volunteer per team — with budgeted time, not “on top of everything.”
  • Mandate: unblock colleagues, harvest wins for the show-and-tell.
  • Explicitly not: enforcement, or reporting on individuals.
Adoption spreads through trusted peers, not memos.

What kills adoption

Four anti-patterns

The mandate

“Everyone uses AI by Q3” → compliance theater. Logins up, behavior unchanged. (You can mandate logins. You can’t mandate habits.)

Tool sprawl

A new tool every month → dabbling, zero habits.

The hero

One enthusiast does everyone’s AI work. When they leave, the capability leaves in their laptop bag.

Silent measurement

Tracking without announcing → trust collapse. Everything measured is announced.
