Why adoption actually stalls  FRAMEWORK

The four frictions

😨 1 · Fear

“I don’t want to be seen learning a ‘junior’ tool for my own job.”

🎭 2 · Identity

“I mastered this craft for 15 years — and that thing does 70% in seconds.”

⚖️ 3 · Trust

Burned once → never again. Or one great demo → blind trust.

🛋️ 4 · Inertia

The old way costs zero willpower. The new one costs willpower for weeks.

None of these are solved by a feature tour — and all four have met your gym membership before.

The psychology  FRAMEWORK — Kahneman & Tversky

Your brain is working as designed

  • Status-quo bias: a proven old workflow feels safe — even when it is measurably slower.
  • Loss aversion: the cost of changing is felt today; the payoff arrives later. Your brain votes today.
The lenses are proven science. Applying them to AI adoption is my professional judgment — labeled as such.

What the data actually says  STUDY

The tool was constant. The behavior was the variable.

~4,800
professionals in field experiments incl. Microsoft (Cui et al., 2024)
+26%
completed tasks on average — with wide variance
↑ most
gains concentrated in those who changed how they work — not the most senior
Averages hide the story: outcomes were decided by working habits. Honest caveat: measured on software teams — the easiest profession to measure. The mechanism is habit change, and habits don’t care about your industry.

Why tool training fails  STUDY — McKinsey 2023

The “give a fish” model

  • Trainer demos features → participants nod → Monday, old habits win. (Undefeated since forever.)
  • McKinsey: productivity gains materialize only with training and practice —
  • and are minimal on complex tasks without changed working patterns.
Knowledge was never the gap. The default behavior is the gap.
