Rewiring the Daily Workflow

Four tools. Four habits. One rule: AI is your first step.

Yesterday we diagnosed. Today we rewire.

The principle

Change the order, not the toolbox

Old: task → do it the old way → (maybe) ask AI to polish the result
→
New: task → AI first draft / first answer → your judgment → done
We are not adding tools to your day. We are changing the order of your day.

Anti-pattern

Ten tools and zero habits
is worse than one tool and one habit

Tool sprawl produces dabbling, decision fatigue, and nothing on Monday.

Rule of the day: leave with one habit per tool — maximum four, and only for tools that fit your work.

Nobody ever got promoted for having the most AI tabs open.

Habit 1 — prompting is a behavior, not a trick

R · T · F

Role

“You are a bid analyst reporting to the CEO…”

Task

“…extract the five requirements we cannot meet today…”

Format

“…as a table: requirement, gap, evidence quote.”

The single highest-leverage habit in this course. The only prompting framework we teach — one frame practiced beats five admired.

RTF — before / after (live)

LAZY

summarize this document

RTF

You are a risk auditor reporting to a CFO. Extract the five largest financial risks from this document. Deliver as a table: risk, likelihood, evidence quote.
Watch the delta live — same document, both prompts. No invented percentages needed; your eyes are the evidence.

Habit 2

Iterate. Don’t regenerate.

  • Refine: “shorter · more formal · add the compliance angle” — instead of re-rolling the dice.
  • Decompose big asks into steps — like you would for a person.
  • Treat it like a brilliant new colleague on day one: brief it, don’t fire it. (Firing a new hire over a first draft? HR would like a word.)

Workflow · finding public bids & tenders

From browsing portals to interrogating them

Old: manually scan tender portals; skim PDFs; hope you didn’t miss one
→
New: assistant searches & shortlists → you verify every deadline, budget and eligibility rule at the source
  • RTF: “You are a bid scout. Find open public tenders for [event services] in [region]. Table: title, buyer, deadline, budget, link.”
  • The link column is mandatory — a shortlist without sources is a rumor with nice formatting.
Deadlines, values, eligibility: verify before anything enters your pipeline — Module 3 makes this a 60-second habit.

Workflow · documents & proposals

Offers, tender responses, reports — AI-first

  • Extract before you write: “List every requirement in this tender as a checklist with the page it came from.”
  • Draft against the checklist: your template + RTF → first version in minutes, not days.
  • Interrogate your own draft: “You are the buyer’s evaluator. Score this response against the checklist — where do we lose points?”
The compliance checklist is the spec — Module 4 turns this instinct into a repeatable template.

Workflow · event materials

Agendas, sponsor decks, social copy — one voice

  • Standing instructions: write your brand voice, audience and no-go phrases once (custom instructions / project) — every draft inherits it.
  • One brief → many artifacts: event one-pager → agenda, sponsor pitch, three social posts, MC script — each a refinement, not a restart.
  • Image & visual briefs: describe the concept precisely; iterate like you iterate text.
  • “Won’t it all sound generic?” Unbriefed AI produces beige — justified fear. The fix: your voice file + your taste as the final gate. AI widens the options; you still pick. (At the kids' playhouse I rejected two designs before picking the third.)
The standing instruction is your first specification — remember this in Module 4.

Tool 2 · Cursor

The codebase conversation

Old: grep + tribal knowledge + coffee
→
New: ask the codebase directly — before writing any code
  • Codebase Q&A first · inline edits · agent mode for scoped multi-file changes.

Rule of engagement  VERIFIED sidebar

The larger the change, the smaller the leash

Inline edit

Review each hunk. Every one.

Agent mode

Review the whole diff like a hostile PR from an unknown contributor.

This working pattern, industrialized: Proof of Scale — 20 legacy apps understood in 3 weeks, 4–8× faster, 187 endpoints mapped. (With verification gates — Module 4 shows the machine.)

Tool 3 · NotebookLM

Grounded learning

Old: the PDF graveyard you’ll never read
→
New: a notebook that answers only from your sources — with citations
  • Onboard to a new domain, client or tender pack · audio overview for the commute · “what do my sources disagree about?”

Why it matters

Evidence-over-opinion, as a product

  • It answers from your uploaded sources and cites the exact passage.
  • Hallucination surface drastically reduced by design.
  • Caveat, honestly: citations shrink the verification job. They don’t delete it.

Tool 4 · Gemini

Scale and modality

Old: “I’ll watch the 90-minute recording later” (never)
→
New: interrogate the video / meeting / spreadsheet directly
  • Enormous inputs (huge context) · video & audio · Google Workspace (Docs, Sheets, Meet).

Choosing without paralysis

The decision tree

Is it code?→ Cursor (IT track)
Is it a pile of your documents?→ NotebookLM
Is it video / audio / enormous?→ Gemini
Thinking, writing, deciding?→ Assistant (Claude / ChatGPT)
When in doubt: start in the assistant, move when a specialist tool earns it. A default beats a debate.
